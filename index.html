I’ve loved to travel for as long as I can remember. It’s not just being someplace different, though I love that part of it. It’s also the journey. I’ve always been drawn to airports and the buzzing energy that exudes from so many travelers being in one place at a time. I also really enjoy flying. It’s almost a spiritual experience to be lifted up into the clouds, even when the ride is a little bumpy. I haven’t really traveled by boat for long distances, but I love being on the water. And put me in a train any day.

I’m not sure why it took so long for us to make traveling a regular part of our lives together, but Ana and I didn’t really go on a trip together until November 2006 (the trip to Germany in 2002 was for my brother's wedding). Perhaps it was because Ana was in school for nearly the first six years of our relationship and I was the only one earning an income. We love to travel—especially together. I’m so glad we’ve made a commitment to make it focus of our relationship.

Of course we’ve visited family, but that isn’t the same as traveling for pleasure. I like to make a distinction between a visit and a trip. Sometimes the two overlap, but we find a way to explore where we are enough to call it (at least partly) a trip. But some places (like where my mother lives in Illinois or where Ana’s grandmother used to live in Florida) just aren’t that interesting, so we end up spending all our time with friends or family.

I’m looking forward to reflecting a bit on where we’ve been. We have a lot of dreams about where we’d still like to go. Until then, looking back will have to suffice.
